what I do.

Today's business environment is becoming increasingly complex and technology-dependent. Uncertainties include demographic and economic shifts, technological innovation, cyber crime, and increased regulation. For an organization to survive, it has to respond with changes to its practices—and changes to business practice can in turn require changes to the organization's complex information technology. Given the omnipresence of technology and our utter dependency on data, we've got a lot riding on the success of these changes.

I work in technology change and delivery.

my clients

Since 1994, my clients and employers have been in regulated industries such as wealth management, capital markets, life insurance, and health. I've worked for startups in broadcasting, telecommunications and networking, entertainment software and even jewelry. My career has taken me to Australia and Japan, and I have worked for employers from four continents.

how I help

I assist firms by implementing new processes and technologies while delivering software and a stable production environment. As the case studies provided here show, I typically tackle projects such as:

  • Giving shape to practices in reliable and secure software delivery, such as prioritizing stories for development, scheduling sprints, and ensuring separate environments for test, integration, UAT, and production.
  • Adopting technologies such as automated configuration and automated testing.
  • Organizing and guiding projects.
  • Leading software and infrastructure teams.
  • Performing analysis, workflow design, and product architecture.

I have studied risk in an effort to understand the complexity and uncertainty that pervade so many projects today. I find agility is key. One of my approaches is to stay truly agile in our activities. By this I don't mean "agile software practices" but simple rules such as:

  • Find out where you are.
  • Take a small step toward your goal.
  • Adjust your understanding, based on what you learned.
  • Repeat.

I’ve also come to believe that the key to delivering technology is not striving for a perfect solution but rather adopting the capacity to rapidly respond as requirements change. To this end I put a lot of energy into establishing a culture by which:

  • Decision-making and risk-taking ability resides where it is most impactful: with the engineers tasked with the solutions.
  • Technical debt is recognized for the innovation killer it is.
  • Issues that arise are treated as the beginning of the problem-solving process, not for finger-pointing.
  • Well-rounded, able people are developed through mentoring, empowerment, and fair dealing.
