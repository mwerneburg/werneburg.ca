what I do.

I believe today's business environment is becoming increasingly complex and technology-dependent. Uncertainties include demographic and economic shifts, a need for constant technological innovation, and increased regulation. For an organization to thrive, it has to respond with changes to its services and practices. Given the omnipresence of technology and our utter dependency on data, these changes in services and practices frequently involve changes to an already-complex information technology environment. This can be as difficult as it is important.

I manage these mission-critical change initiatives.

how I help

I assist firms by implementing new processes and technologies while delivering software and a stable production environment. As the case studies provided here show, I typically tackle projects such as:

  • Supporting new business lines with technology architecture, development and deployments.
  • Taking software delivery processes from the ad-hoc to the repeatable and efficient.
  • Blending risk- or audit-driven control requirements with existing software processes.

my approach

I have studied risk in an effort to understand the complexity and uncertainty that pervade so many projects today. I find agility is key. By this I don't mean "agile software practices" but simple loop:

  • Find out where you are.
  • Orient toward your goal and take a small step toward it.
  • Understand any new information that came to light.
  • Repeat.

I've come to believe that the key to delivering technology is not striving for a perfect solution but rather adopting the capacity to rapidly respond as requirements change. To this end I put a lot of energy into establishing a culture by which:

  • Decision-making and risk-taking ability resides where it is most impactful: with the engineers tasked with the solutions.
  • Efficient automated processes speed testing and stable software delivery.
  • Technical debt is recognized for the innovation killer it is and is eliminated with every opportunity.
  • Issues that arise are treated as the beginning of the problem-solving process, not for finger-pointing.
  • Well-rounded, able people are developed through mentoring, empowerment, and fair dealing.
